Alabama Occupational Licensing

RBIJ mobilized businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that expands access to occupational licensing in Alabama by:

  • Sign-on statement
  • Private advocacy
  • Op-ed: Elizabeth Anderson, CEO of Lunar Labs
  • Blog: STRIVE on their Alabama Program

In May 2025, Governor Ivey signed this legislation into law, ensuring fairer access to licensed jobs, reduced recidivism, and an expansion of Alabama’s economy.

Stats

~900,000 Alabamians — 22% of the state’s adult population have some form of arrest or conviction record.

In Alabama, 1 in 5 workers must obtain a license for fields as varied as plumbing, dentistry, nursing, cosmetology, and law.

There are only 38 available workers for every 100 open jobs.
