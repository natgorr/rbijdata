Alabama Occupational Licensing

RBIJ mobilized businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that expands access to occupational licensing in Alabama by:

  • Sign-on statement
  • Private Advocacy

In May 2025, Governor Ivey signed this legislation into law, ensuring fairer access to licensed jobs, reduced recidivism, and an expansion of Alabama’s economy.

Past Wins

  • Approximately 900,000 Alabamians — 22% of the state’s adult population — have some form of arrest or conviction record.
  • In Alabama, one in five workers are required to obtain a license for fields as varied as plumbing, dentistry, nursing, cosmetology, and law.
  • At the same time, there are only 38 available workers for every 100 open jobs.
