Fair Checks (UK only)

In the U.K., more than 12.6 million people have a criminal record, including 1 in 4 working-age people. These are mostly for offenses that were minor or happened a long time ago. Many of these individuals cannot access stable employment and are prevented from providing for themselves and their families.

FairChecks would create a fairer and more proportionate system that allows people to move on, find work and contribute to society. FairChecks would help rebuild workforces, strengthen communities, meet ongoing labour shortages and drive inclusive growth, by:

  • Eliminating automatic disclosure of cautions
  • Wiping the slate clean for childhood offenses
  • Excluding short or suspended prison sentences after a certain period
