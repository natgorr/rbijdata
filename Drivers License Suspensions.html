Reforming Driver’s License Suspensions

Most states suspend, revoke, or refuse to renew driver’s licenses for unpaid fines and fees. They also criminalize driving on a suspended license, and suspend licenses for failures to appear in court.

Loss of a license leaves those who drive to work with an impossible choice: lose their jobs or risk further penalties — even arrest — to keep their income. Simply put, it forces Americans into a vicious and desperate cycle of poverty and incarceration.

Ending driver’s license suspensions for unpaid fines and fees will keep workers in their jobs and out of the justice system.
