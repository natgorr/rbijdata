Past Wins

Ohio: Driver’s License Reform

RBIJ recruited and engaged businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that ends debt-based driver’s license suspensions in Ohio by:

  • Business sign-on letter
  • Testimony from seven business leaders in support
  • Private advocacy to key legislators
  • Op-ed with Phil Petrill (CEO of Hot Chicken Takeover) as byline in the Columbus Dispatch

The bill was signed into law in January 2025 and will help keep Ohioans on the road, gainfully employed, and contributing to society.

Ohio: Access to Identification (ID bill)

RBIJ mobilized businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that would help returning citizens in Ohio access identification documentation and provide resume writing services and interview practice by:

  • Public testimony from local chambers and business leaders
  • Private advocacy to key legislators

In December 2024, the bill was signed into law by Governor DeWine, ensuring that individuals can more easily find employment and education opportunities immediately upon their release.

Stats

82% of Ohioans rely on a car to get to and from work.

Ohio sees roughly 3 million license suspensions per year with over 60% being for debt-related reasons, such as a missed traffic ticket.

Black drivers are 31% more likely to be pulled over than white drivers and are disproportionately ticketed, arrested, charged, and convicted for traffic violations, as well as for driving with suspended licenses.

Restoring 7,000 licenses through a program that helped drivers repay their debt led to an estimated GDP increase of $149.6 million.

So far, 7,076 drivers have regained their valid driver’s license under this law; $8,380,681 in reinstatement fees were waived.

Every year, 18,000 Ohioans exit prison and re-enter their communities.

2.1 million Ohioans — 23% of the state’s adult population — have some form of arrest or conviction record.
