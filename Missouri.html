Missouri: Clean Slate

RBIJ is currently engaging local business leaders in Missouri around the state’s ongoing Clean Slate effort. To date, this includes:

  • Business sign-on letter with more than 20 supportive businesses
  • Public testimony on behalf of 11 businesses
  • Private advocacy to key legislators
  • Op-eds: Jackie Huebbe (Owner of SugarBot Bakery), Tom Lesnak (President, Independence Chamber of Commerce)
  • Speaking opportunities: Channel 4 News

Stats

1.9 million Missourians — nearly one-third of the state’s total population — have some form of arrest or conviction record.

While half a million Missourians are currently eligible to have their record cleared (“expunged”), less than 1% of eligible individuals complete the process due to a lack of access, knowledge, and funds required.

As a result, the state loses billions in cumulative earnings every year due to underemployment from convictions that could be, but have not been, cleared.

The Missouri Budget Project estimates a $2.9 billion economic impact annually from Clean Slate, including a $2.3 billion increase in annual wages.
