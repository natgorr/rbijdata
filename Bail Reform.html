Bail Reform

After an arrest, an individual's ability to leave jail, return home, and maintain steady employment typically depends on their ability to provide bail. Bail is the temporary release of a person awaiting trial, often on the condition that money be given to guarantee their appearance in court.

On any given day, more than 600,000 Americans are incarcerated in local jails alone. Over the past three decades, the number of annual jail admissions doubled, and the average length of stay increased from 14 to 23 days.

Reforming our bail system will strengthen the economy, improve long-term community safety, and increase justice and equity for our communities.
