Stats

3.3 million adults in Illinois have been arrested or convicted of a crime since 1979; 2.2 million of these individuals are eligible to have their records sealed right now.

Only 10% of eligible Illinoisans pursue the current record sealing process, hindered by limited knowledge, legal support, and funds.

As a result, the state loses an estimated $4.7 billion in cumulative earnings every year due to convictions that could be, but have not been, cleared.
