Current Activity

Georgia: Occupational Licensing

RBIJ is currently engaging local business leaders in Georgia around the state's ongoing effort to expand access to occupational licensing. To date, this includes:

  • Business sign-on letter with more than a dozen business leaders
  • Private advocacy to key legislators
  • Public testimony on behalf of businesses
  • Op-eds and media: Charlotte Garnes (ReNForce), The Atlanta Journal-Constitution

Stats

In Georgia, one in every seven jobs requires an occupational license — as does one in every four of the state’s “high demand” jobs.

Nearly 3 million Georgians — 36% of the state’s adult population — have some form of arrest or conviction record.
