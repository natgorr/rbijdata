Tennessee: Occupational Licensing

RBIJ mobilized businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that would expand access to occupational licensing in Tennessee by:

  • Private advocacy to key legislators
  • Public testimony

In April 2024, HB1859 was signed into law by Governor Lee, ensuring fairer access to licensed jobs, reduced recidivism, and an expansion of Tennessee’s economy.

Stats

In Tennessee, more than one in five workers across the labor force are required to obtain a license for fields as varied as plumbing, dentistry, nursing, cosmetology, and law.

1.5 million Tennesseans — 29% of the state’s adult population — have some form of arrest or conviction record.
