Ending Unjust Fines & Fees

Every year, millions of Americans are burdened by court-imposed fines and fees. Originally intended to punish and fund the justice system, the use of exorbitant fines and fees has steadily increased over the past decade — often reaching hundreds or thousands of dollars.

Those who cannot immediately pay face harsh penalties, including arrest, incarceration, license suspensions, additional fees, and even loss of voting rights. These costs block justice-impacted individuals from steady employment and limit employers’ ability to hire. They trap entire families in cycles of poverty and punishment, thwarting economic mobility.

Eliminating unnecessary, ineffective fines and fees in our justice system strengthens the workforce and helps ensure justice-impacted individuals can continue to work, support their families, and meaningfully contribute to their communities.
