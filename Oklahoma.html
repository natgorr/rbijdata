Oklahoma: Fines & Fees Reform

RBIJ mobilized businesses — and their leaders — to publicly and privately support legislation that eliminates harmful and unnecessary fees in Oklahoma’s justice system by:

  • Sign-on statement
  • Private advocacy to key legislators
  • Webinar co-hosted by Small Business Majority and RBIJ, featuring First Lady of Oklahoma, Sarah Stitt
  • Op-ed: Melissa Walton, Journal Record

The bill was signed into law by Governor Stitt in May 2025, allowing for successful re-entry of those impacted by the justice system.

Stats

Oklahoma wastes limited resources trying to collect unnecessary fees: it costs 121 times more money to collect criminal justice fees than to collect tax revenue.

Fees are a widely recognized barrier to successful reentry; 2 in 5 people report resorting to illegal activity to get the money needed to resolve their debt.
